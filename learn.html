What is AI?

⏱ 10 min +50 XP Beginner

Artificial Intelligence is the field of computer science dedicated to building systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence — things like understanding language, recognizing images, making decisions, and learning from experience.

Two flavors of AI

When people talk about AI today, they usually mean one of two things:

Narrow AI — systems that are exceptionally good at one specific task. Every AI product you use today (ChatGPT, Spotify recommendations, Google Translate) is narrow AI.
General AI (AGI) — a hypothetical system with human-level intelligence across all domains. It doesn't exist yet, but it's what most AI researchers are working toward.

AI vs. Automation

A common misconception is that AI and automation are the same thing. They're not. A dishwasher automates washing dishes — it follows a fixed script. An AI system learns from data and can generalize to new situations it wasn't explicitly programmed for.

# Automation: explicit rules
def is_spam(email):
    if "FREE MONEY" in email:
        return True
    return False

# AI: learned from examples
model = train_on(10_000_labeled_emails)
model.predict(new_email)  # generalizes!

That difference — learning vs. scripting — is the core of what makes AI powerful and unpredictable.

Common Mistake: Thinking "AI" means humanoid robots. Almost all real-world AI is invisible software running behind apps and websites.
